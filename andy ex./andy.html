I was born and raised in Salt Lake City, Utah. Growing up, I spent a lot of time kayaking, snowboarding, backpacking, and hiking with my family (Utah an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise). You could also often find me with my nose buried in a book, in front of the TV with my face glued to the PlayStation, or jamming on my guitar with the amp cranked to 11.

Feeling a little pent up in Utah and seeking adventure, I flew across the country to go to college in beautiful Ithaca, NY after high school, majoring in English Lit and minoring in German. I sang bass and beatboxed in an a cappella choir, built a house in Georgia with Habitat for Humanity, and spent a lot of late nights in the library reading Aeschylus and memorizing verb declination charts.

After graduation I was awarded a Fulbright grant to Hamburg, Germany and worked as an assistant English teacher there for a year. I ran around with a big group of other European expats and had a fantastic time exploring the beautiful city of Hamburg, improving my German, and working with my students (who affectionately yelled out Meestah Frai! and demanded that I beatbox for them whenever they saw me between classes or around town).

I did some traveling after my Fulbright, and walked the Camino de Santiago, a centuries-old pilgrimage route spanning over 500 miles east to west across northern Spain. I walked the whole thing, step by step, over the course of a month, and shared some wonderful moments and laughs with the amazing fellow pilgrims I met on the way. I was rail thin by the end, and literally had blisters on blisters on my feet.

...and since then I’ve been living and working in New York! I’ve done a lot so far in the city: I’ve worked as a bartender in Times Square, as a sales assistant at a midtown TV ad firm, and most recently as a freelance German-English translator and proofreader. In my free time I love to explore Brooklyn on my bike, discover new restaurants with my girlfriend, and toss a Frisbee around in Prospect Park when the weather is nice.
